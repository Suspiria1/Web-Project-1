♟️Chess Notation♟️

Chess notation are a combination of letters and numbers to keep track of chess moves. Today, we use algebraic notation. Every square on the board has a notation. The notation has the letter of the file, and the number of the rank. In algebraic notation, we also use capital letters for the pieces("K" for king, "Q" for queen, "R" for rook, "B" for bishop, and "N" for knight). When you capture a piece we use the letter "x". Castling short(kingside) is represented by "0-0" and castling long(queenside) is represented by "0-0-0".
